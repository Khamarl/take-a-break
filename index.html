Why are breaks so important?

Fun Fact 1

Time away from a task also allows you to come back with heightened concentration levels, making it easier to memorise new information.

Fun Fact 2

Social interactions during a break allow you to share your experiences and feel part of a group.

Fun Fact 3

Regular breaks provide an opportunity to destress, keeping performance high as well as minimising the risk of fatigue and mental strain.

Fun Fact 4

Being hungry leads to poor decision making and procrastination.

Fun Fact 5

Rest and sleep have been shown to have a positive impact on your immune system. Taking regular breaks can reduce inflammation and decrease the risk of heart disease.

Fun Fact 6

A 10 minute break gives you plenty of time to go for a quick walk, taking in a change of scenery and helping to refresh your mind.

The science behind taking a break

The prefrontal cortex is responsible for logical thinking, executive funtioning and keeping focused on your goals. That's a lot of responsibilityâ€”no wonder it needs a break!

Studies have shown that taking a break is linked to:

  • Improved life quality: sitting for prolonged periods reduces blood flow to the legs and increases the risk of heart disease.
  • Preventing decision fatigue: a study showed that judges were more likely to grant parole when taking a break after working.
  • Restoring motivation: briefly taking your mind off a goal can strengthen your motivation and focus towards that task.
